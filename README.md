# Computing and AI Ethics
**Brendan Shea,PhD**
Classs notes for computing and AI Ethics

## Table of Contents
1. Introduction to Ethics in Computing and AI (overview of issues, Five-Principles framework)
2. Technology and the Virtues (Shannon Vallor and Jonathan Haidt on Virtues ih a world of Social Media/AI)
3. From Frankenstein to Murderbot -- How science fiction can help us think about the social and ethical implications of information, technology -- Frankenstein, 1984, Dune, Neuromancer, Matrix, Murderbot.
4. The Copyright Conundrum (How IP is--and isnt'--like physical property? Why it's tought to get the balance rigtht -- on everythihg from DMCA to right to repair to LLM model training)
5. The Promise and Peril of Predictive AI (explain machine learning, general problems of overfit models not working well, combined with bias. AI Snake Oil book, Paul Meehl, replication crisis and more!)
6. Is Generative AI Bullshit? (Explain LLMs and link "hallucinations" to Frankfurt's idea of bullshit. Explain ideas on how to fix this).
7. Free Speech and Content Moderation Online (Philosophical introduction to free speech debates; why even well-performing AI couldn't solve this problem for us).
8. The Enshittification Problem (why/how increasing market power in tech has led to buyers, sellers, and workers being less well-served. Cory Doctorow stuff, but teach students the underlying economics along the way)
9. The information technology of money (a history of money from ancient times to the near present, with a focus on the interplay between finance, society, and information technologies. The invention of writing! Arithmetic! Crime! Marriage! And everthing else).
10. Cryptocurrency and the Future of Money (a continuations of the previous lecture, focused on current debates over cryptocurrency and related technologies.)
11. The Privacy of Puzzle (the many types of privacy and their complex relationships with changing IT).
12. Cute Robots, Killer Robots (What will future AIs be like? HOw can we respect their moral standing? How can we make sure they respect ours).
