Computing and AI Ethics

Brendan Shea, PhD  ·  Rochester Community and Technical College

CC BY-NC 4.0 — Free to use & adapt

This textbook explores the ethical, social, and philosophical questions raised by information technology and artificial intelligence. Each chapter is grounded in the history of ideas, introducing key thinkers and frameworks alongside real-world case studies. The text is designed for introductory courses at the high school or early college level — no prior philosophy or computer science background required.

Chapters are adapted from lecture notes first taught at Rochester Community and Technical College in Spring 2026. Source materials and PDFs are available at github.com/brendanpshea/computing_ai_ethics. Questions and feedback: brendanpshea@gmail.com.

Chapters

Chapter 1 The History of IT Ethics

Why does the history of technology matter for ethics? From writing and the printing press to the telegraph and the internet, each information revolution brought new freedoms — and new dangers. We trace the thinkers who wrestled with each transition.

Read Chapter 1
Chapter 2 Virtue Ethics and Modern Technology

What does it mean to live well in a digital world? Drawing on Aristotle's virtue ethics and contemporary thinkers like Shannon Vallor, this chapter asks which character traits help us use technology wisely — and which habits undermine our flourishing.

Read Chapter 2
Chapter 3 Free Speech in the Digital Age

Should online speech be regulated, and by whom? We examine Mill's harm principle, the boundaries of free expression, content moderation, misinformation, and the special challenges posed by social media platforms that are neither purely public nor purely private.

Read Chapter 3
Chapter 4 Intellectual Property

Who owns an idea — and should anyone? Copyright, patents, open-source licensing, and AI-generated content all depend on contested assumptions about creativity and property. This chapter untangles the competing interests at stake.

Read Chapter 4
Chapter 5 Cryptography and Security

Encryption is simultaneously a tool of privacy, commerce, dissent, and crime. We explore the history and mathematics of cryptography, the ethics of hacking, cybersecurity policy, and the perennial tension between security and civil liberties.

Read Chapter 5
Chapter 6 Privacy

In an age of surveillance capitalism and data brokers, what remains of privacy? This chapter examines philosophical justifications for privacy, the mechanics of data collection, legal frameworks from GDPR to HIPAA, and the ethics of surveillance.

Read Chapter 6
Chapter 7 AI Ethics

How should we build, deploy, and govern artificial intelligence systems? Topics include algorithmic bias, transparency, autonomous weapons, AI in healthcare, and the long-run question of what advanced AI might mean for human autonomy and dignity.

Read Chapter 7
Chapter 8 Work and Automation

Automation has always transformed labor — but is today's AI-driven wave different in kind? We examine economic theories of technological unemployment, the gig economy, universal basic income, and what work means beyond its economic function.

Read Chapter 8
