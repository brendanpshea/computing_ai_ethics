About this course. These are materials for Computing and AI Ethics, first taught at Rochester Community and Technical College in Spring 2026. The course introduces ethical, social, and philosophical questions raised by information technology and artificial intelligence — no prior philosophy or CS background required.

Questions and feedback: brendanpshea@gmail.com  ·  View source on GitHub

A note on these materials. Lecture slides were written by Brendan Shea in LaTeX. The HTML chapter readings were derived from those slides with assistance from large language models — primarily Claude (Anthropic). All content has been reviewed and edited by the author. Both the slides and the HTML readings are released under CC BY-NC 4.0: free to use and adapt for non-commercial purposes with attribution.
